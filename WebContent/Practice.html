How It Works

How to offer a Ride.

Create a Ride

Enter your start and end locations, select the route, enter the start time, cost per seat and done!

Get Request

See requests from potential Riders, view their profiles and pickup/drop points and make them a part of your ride!

Ride Along

Begin your ride, pickup Riders and help them reach their destinations. Make new friends, Share the costs of fuel, reduce pollution and carbon dioxide emissions.


How to take a Ride.

Search for rides

Enter your start and end locations, type of ride i.e. daily or monthly, start time and distance flexibility and view the potential rides on a map with pricings.

Send requests

View driver and the route details and send requests for the rides you feel will be best for you. Once the Offeror accepts your request, sit back and relax!

Ride Along

Get notified when the ride is started, get picked up and reach your destination on time!

Helps You Save Money

The art is not in making money, but in keeping it.

Carpooling allows you to share the cost of gas and parking, cutting your expenses by nearly 50% or more; the more occupants in your carpool the more you save. Carpooling is also socially economical. Not only will you be saving, but you will also help reduce the costs we all pay towards the construction of new roads, road maintenance and air pollution related health costs.

Helps the Environment

Be a part of the Solution not part of Pollution.

Carpooling cuts down on the number of cars and vehicle son the road. Fewer cars means there is less carbon and other gasses and pollution getting into the air. This protects the environment by keeping the air, water, and land cleaner.

Every car annually emits its own weight in carbon dioxide into the atmosphere. Carpooling reduces that amount significantly. If you carpool to and from work, you could probably cut your transportation emissions number in half, or even better.

Its good for your health

Your body is ground zero in the battle over pollution.

According to Environment Canada, air pollution caused by vehicular travel is linked to a number of health concerns including respiratory diseases, cardiovascular disease, allergies and neurological effects.

By carpooling, you help reduce these health risks for yourself and everyone else. Research also suggests that carpooling is less stressful than commuting alone.

Solves Public Transport Problems

Public transport suits the needs of the authority ... not the commuter.

Local trains, metros, buses with endless queues and herds are no one's idea of comfort.

By Vehicle sharing one can get the comfort of a car journey without having to spend much. On public transport instead of chatting with the person sat next to us , we more often than not prefer to be alone on our phones. Car-sharing enables us to communicate with others, to meet people we possibly would never have met.

Helps You Make New Friends

1. Its the friends we meet along the way that help us appreciate journey.
2.Enjoy making new friends along the road.

If the idea of travelling alone only with your Ipod's shuffle button to console you along the way seems a little blue to you, you know what you must do. Not only does carpooling keep you company during a ride but helps you meet people from your own city or a fellow traveler.

Carpooling is a wonderful way to meet interesting people, get to know the people you work with or go to school with, and to make new friends!

Reduce Traffic

Life is too short for traffic.

The benefits of carpooling on a large scale are huge. Every carpooling participant takes another car off the road,which means less congested roads and highways. As more people take part in carpooling programs or organize carpools within their workplaces, overall traffic congestion will decrease, which reduces fuel consumption, commute time, and the cost of road repairs.
